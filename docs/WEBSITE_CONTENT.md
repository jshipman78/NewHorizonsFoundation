## PAGE: ABOUT — MISSION & VALUES

### Mission Statement

**Full Mission:**
> To expand access to ethical mental health services, support those who serve our communities, foster cultural and relational resilience, and develop future leaders through education, collaboration, and service.

---

### Aspirational Ethics

**Introduction:**
> New Horizons Foundation operates from an aspirational ethical framework that guides all programs, partnerships, and governance decisions.

**Core Ethical Principles:**

1. **Compassion in Action**
   > Meeting the needs of individuals and communities with empathy, presence, and practical support.

2. **Faithful Stewardship**
   > Responsible management of resources, relationships, and the trust placed in us by donors, partners, and those we serve.

3. **Integrity in Leadership**
   > Leading with honesty, transparency, and ethical decision-making at every level of the organization.

4. **Responsibility to Others**
   > Prioritizing the well-being of those we serve and the communities we partner with above organizational self-interest.

5. **Hope for Restoration and Growth**
   > Believing in the capacity for healing, change, and positive transformation for individuals, families, and communities.

## PAGE: OUR DIVISIONS

### Division 1: Mental Health — Delivery & Services

**Purpose:**
> To increase access to ethical, evidence-based mental health care and education.

**Scope of Work:**
> This division supports community mental health services, clinical education, prevention-based programming, and professional development. Programs emphasize ethical practice, cultural humility, and integration with existing community resources.

**Key Focus Areas:**
- Community mental health services
- Clinical education and training
- Prevention-based programming
- Professional development
- Ethical practice standards
- Cultural humility in care

**Leadership Oversight:**
> Licensed clinicians and educators with extensive experience in clinical mental health counseling, supervision, and training.

---

### Division 2: Veterans & First Responders

**Purpose:**
> To support the mental health and well-being of veterans, law enforcement, firefighters, EMTs, and their families.

**Scope of Work:**
> Programs focus on education, prevention, resilience, family support, and collaboration between mental health professionals and public-safety agencies. Special attention is given to occupational stress, trauma exposure, and moral injury.

**Key Focus Areas:**
- Education and awareness
- Prevention and resilience programs
- Family support services
- Public-safety agency collaboration
- Occupational stress support
- Trauma and moral injury resources

**Leadership Oversight:**
> Professionals experienced in community engagement, crisis response, and public-safety collaboration.

---

### Division 3: Cultural Enrichment & Community Healing

**Purpose:**
> To strengthen mental health outcomes by honoring culture, identity, and shared meaning.

**Scope of Work:**
> This division integrates culturally informed education, community dialogue, and intergenerational programming into mental health outreach. Programs aim to reduce stigma and foster belonging through respectful engagement with diverse communities.

**Key Focus Areas:**
- Culturally informed education
- Community dialogue programs
- Intergenerational programming
- Stigma reduction initiatives
- Belonging and community connection
- Diverse community engagement

**Leadership Oversight:**
> Experts in culturally responsive mental health and community-based healing approaches.

---

### Division 4: Youth Leadership & Education

**Purpose:**
> To develop the next generation of leaders grounded in character, responsibility, and service.

**Scope of Work:**
> Programs focus on emotional intelligence, leadership development, mentorship, service-based learning, and family involvement. Youth leadership initiatives are designed to cultivate purpose, resilience, and ethical decision-making.

**Key Focus Areas:**
- Emotional intelligence development
- Leadership skills training
- Mentorship programs
- Service-based learning
- Family involvement initiatives
- Character and ethics education

**Leadership Oversight:**
> Educators and clinicians with experience in youth development, leadership training, and mentorship.
